# Network-analysis-of-Comorbidity-of-Anxiety-and-Depression-of-CAD-patients

Bayesian Gaussian Graphical Models (BGGM) of depression and anxiety symptom networks across five post-cardiac-event time points, with Bayesian Model Averaging (BMA), community detection, temporal change detection, and subgroup comparisons.

**OVERVIEW **
The goal of the analysis is to estimate and compare symptom-level networks of depression (PHQ-9) and anxiety (GAD-7) in cardiac patients from the THORESCI cohort. Patients are measured at five waves (i.e, baseline, 1 month, 6 months, 12 months, and 24 months) with a separate partial polychoric correlation network being estimated for each wave. The goal is to characterize how the structure of depression–anxiety comorbidity (which symptoms connect to which, and how strongly) evolves over time and differs across patient subgroups (e.g., women vs men, cardiac history vs no cardiac history).

The analysis covers: 
* data pre-processing steps
* descriptives and reliability estimates
* (subgroup) network BMA estimation
* temporal changes
* community detection via Clique Percolation (CP)
* Visualization
* network descriptive metrics (added after pre-registration of the study)
